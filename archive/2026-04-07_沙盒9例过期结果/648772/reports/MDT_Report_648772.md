### Recommended Treatment Plan

#### **Local Therapy (Post-operative)**
**Stereotactic Radiosurgery (SRS) to Resection Cavity**
- **Dose:** 27 Gy in 3 fractions (fractionated SRS preferred for larger cavities) [Local: EANOeESMO_Clinical_Practice_Guidelines_for_diagnosis_treatment_and_follow_up_of_patients_with_brain_metastasis_from_solid_tumours.md, Section: STEREOTACTIC RADIOSURGERY]
- **Rationale:** Post-operative SRS significantly improves freedom from local recurrence compared to observation alone [PubMed: PMID 28592387]
- **Timing:** Within 4-6 weeks post-surgery (after wound healing)

**Evidence:**
- EANO-ESMO guidelines recommend SRS to resection cavity after complete resection of brain metastases [Local: EANOeESMO_Clinical_Practice_Guidelines_for_diagnosis_treatment_and_follow_up_of_patients_with_brain_metastasis_from_solid_tumours.md, Section: RECOMMENDATIONS - RADIOTHERAPY]
- Brown et al. (NCCTG N107C/CEC.3): Post-operative SRS vs WBRT showed no OS difference but less cognitive decline with SRS [PubMed: PMID 28592387 - referenced in guideline]
- Mahajan et al.: Post-operative SRS vs observation showed significantly improved freedom from local recurrence [Local: EANOeESMO_Clinical_Practice_Guidelines_for_diagnosis_treatment_and_follow_up_of_patients_with_brain_metastasis_from_solid_tumours.md, Section: STEREOTACTIC RADIOSURGERY]

#### **Systemic Therapy (First-line)**

**Option A: If BRAF V600 Mutation-Positive**
- **Preferred:** Ipilimumab + Nivolumab (anti-CTLA4 + anti-PD1 combination)
  - **Dose:** Nivolumab 1 mg/kg + Ipilimumab 3 mg/kg IV every 3 weeks × 4 doses, then Nivolumab maintenance [PubMed: PMID 30045186]
  - **Evidence:** EANO-ESMO guidelines recommend Ipi-Nivo as preferred first-line even in BRAF-mutated asymptomatic patients [Local: EANOeESMO_Clinical_Practice_Guidelines_for_diagnosis_treatment_and_follow_up_of_patients_with_brain_metastasis_from_solid_tumours.md, Section: PHARMACOTHERAPY - BM FROM MELANOMA]

- **Alternative (if symptomatic or high steroid requirement):** Dabrafenib + Trametinib

  - **Evidence:** COMBI-MB trial showed intracranial response rates up to 60% in BRAF-mutated melanoma brain metastases [Local: EANOeESMO_Clinical_Practice_Guidelines_for_diagnosis_treatment_and_follow_up_of_patients_with_brain_metastasis_from_solid_tumours.md, Section: PHARMACOTHERAPY - BM FROM MELANOMA]
