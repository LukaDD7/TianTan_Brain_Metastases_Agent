## primary_plan (首选方案)

### Recommended Treatment Strategy

#### 1. Systemic Pharmacotherapy (First-Line)
**Preferred regimen**: Ipilimumab + Nivolumab combination immunotherapy
- **Dosing**: Standard dosing per institutional protocol
- **Evidence level**: EANO: II, B; ESMO: II, B
- **Rationale**: Current data favour the combination with an overall response rate of ~50%, reasonable response duration and PFS of >50% at 18 months in asymptomatic brain metastases [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- **Note**: Efficacy is lower in patients with symptomatic brain metastasis requiring steroids (21% intracranial response rates) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]

#### 2. Local Therapy Options

**Option A: Stereotactic Radiosurgery (SRS)**
- **Indication**: Patients with limited number (1-4) of brain metastases
- **Evidence level**: EANO: I, A; ESMO: I, A
- **Dosing**: Single fraction 15-24 Gy, or fractionated SRS (27 Gy in three fractions or 30 Gy in five fractions) for lesions >3 cm or near critical structures [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5, 7]
- May be considered for 5-10 brain metastases with cumulative tumour volume <15 ml (EANO: II, B; ESMO: II, B) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]

**Option B: Surgical Resection**
- **Indications**:
  - Single brain metastasis (EANO: I, A; ESMO: II, A) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
  - Multiple resectable brain metastases (EANO: IV, C; ESMO: V, C) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
  - Acute symptoms of raised intracranial pressure (EANO: III, C; ESMO: IV, B) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
  - Patient requires steroids and is candidate for immune checkpoint inhibition (EANO: III, n/a; ESMO: IV, B) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]

  - Posterior fossa location due to risk of obstructive hydrocephalus [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 4]

**Post-operative Management**:

- Post-operative SRS to resection cavity recommended (EANO: I, A; ESMO: I, A) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]

## systemic_management (全身管理)

### Immunotherapy Management
**Ipilimumab + Nivolumab Combination**:
- Preferred first-line treatment for asymptomatic patients regardless of BRAF status (EANO: II, B; ESMO: II, B) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- Monitor for immune-related adverse events
- Steroid use may negatively impact immunotherapy outcomes [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5]

### BRAF-Targeted Therapy (if BRAF-mutated)
**For BRAF V600-mutated melanoma**:
- BRAF inhibitors (vemurafenib or dabrafenib): intracranial response rates 15-40% [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- BRAF + MEK inhibitor combination (dabrafenib + trametinib): intracranial response rates up to 60% in asymptomatic untreated brain metastases [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- **Indication for dabrafenib + trametinib**: Patients with multiple symptomatic BRAF-mutated brain metastases or requiring ≥4 mg dexamethasone (EANO: IV, B; ESMO: IV, B) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]

### Steroid Management
- Use lowest dose for shortest time possible when required for raised intracranial pressure (EANO: IV, n/a; ESMO: IV, B) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 12]
- Steroids only in symptomatic patients (EANO: IV, n/a; ESMO: IV, B) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 12]
- Preliminary evidence suggests negative impact on immunotherapy outcomes [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5]
- Surgery allows rapid steroid tapering and optimises immunotherapy efficacy [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 4]

### Seizure Management
- Primary anticonvulsant prophylaxis NOT recommended (EANO: I, A; ESMO: I, A) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 12]
- Patients with seizures: secondary prophylaxis with levetiracetam, lamotrigine or lacosamide (avoid phenytoin, carbamazepine, valproic acid due to drug interactions) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 12]

### Thromboprophylaxis
- Primary thromboprophylaxis in hospitalised or bed-confined patients
- LMWH recommended (EANO: II, B; ESMO: II, C) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 12]
- Risk of intracranial bleeding not increased with therapeutic LMWH doses [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 12]

### Pneumocystis jirovecii Pneumonia Prophylaxis
- Consider trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole if steroids > few weeks with additional immunosuppressive therapy [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 12]

## rejected_alternatives (被排除方案)

### 1. Post-operative Whole-Brain Radiotherapy (WBRT)
**Reason for exclusion**: Post-operative WBRT after neurosurgical resection or SRS should be discouraged (EANO: I, A; ESMO: I, E) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- Randomised trials showed no OS benefit compared to SRS alone
- More frequent cognitive decline after WBRT [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5]
- No benefit demonstrated in melanoma patients already locally treated for 1-3 brain metastases [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5]

### 2. Systemic Chemotherapy (temozolomide, dacarbazine, fotemustine)
**Reason for exclusion**: Has only limited efficacy in melanoma patients with brain metastases [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- Inferior to immunotherapy and targeted therapy options

### 3. Single-agent Immunotherapy (anti-PD-1 monotherapy)
**Reason for exclusion**: Ipilimumab + nivolumab combination preferred based on superior overall response rate (~50% vs lower rates with monotherapy) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- Combination shows better response duration and PFS

### 4. Primary Anticonvulsant Prophylaxis
**Reason for exclusion**: Not recommended as not effective in preventing first-ever seizure (EANO: I, A; ESMO: I, A) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 12]

### 5. Prophylactic Cranial Irradiation (PCI)
**Reason for exclusion**: Not indicated for melanoma; PCI established only for small-cell lung cancer [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5, 7]

### 6. WBRT as Initial Treatment for Limited Brain Metastases
**Reason for exclusion**: SRS is standard of care for 1-4 brain metastases (EANO: I, A; ESMO: I, A) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- WBRT associated with neurocognitive decline without OS benefit [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5]

- **Steroid optimization**: Use lowest effective dose; plan for rapid post-operative tapering to optimize immunotherapy [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 4, 12]

### Intra-operative Parameters
- **Extent of resection**: Gross total resection associated with improved local control (EANO: I, A; ESMO: II, A) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 4]
- **En bloc resection**: May result in lower recurrence rates and lower risk of leptomeningeal dissemination than piecemeal resections [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 4]

### molecular_pathology_orders (分子病理送检医嘱)
**Required Molecular Testing on Resected Tissue**:
1. **BRAF V600E/K mutation testing** (critical for melanoma treatment selection)
2. **NRAS mutation testing** (Q61, G12, G13 codons)
3. **c-KIT mutation testing** (especially for acral/mucosal melanoma subtypes)

### Post-operative Parameters
- **MRI within 48 hours**: Post-operative MRI to determine extent of resection (EANO: IV, C; ESMO: V, C) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 4, 7]
- **Steroid tapering**: Rapid taper to optimize immunotherapy efficacy [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 4]
- **SRS timing**: Post-operative SRS to resection cavity recommended (EANO: I, A; ESMO: I, A) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
- **Risk-adapted fractionation**: For larger resection cavities, distribute total dose over 3-5 fractions [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5]

### Contraindications to Surgery
- Uncontrolled systemic disease with expected survival <3 months
- >10 brain metastases (relative contraindication; consider SRS if cumulative volume <15 ml)
- Poor performance status not allowing recovery
- Lesions in surgically inaccessible locations (consider SRS instead) [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 8]

### Evidence Levels Applied
- **Level I, A**: Post-operative WBRT discouraged; SRS for 1-4 brain metastases; post-operative SRS to cavity; primary anticonvulsant prophylaxis not recommended
- **Level II, B**: Ipilimumab + nivolumab preferred first-line; SRS for 5-10 brain metastases with volume <15 ml; LMWH for thromboprophylaxis
- **Level III, B**: WBRT for multiple brain metastases not amenable to SRS; surgery for raised intracranial pressure
- **Level IV, B**: Dabrafenib + trametinib for symptomatic BRAF-mutated brain metastases; brain MRI every 2-3 months
- **Level IV, C**: Post-operative MRI within 48 hours; advanced MRI techniques for distinguishing progression

### Decision Rationale
1. Ipilimumab + nivolumab selected as first-line based on ~50% overall response rate and durable responses in asymptomatic brain metastases [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
2. SRS preferred over WBRT for limited brain metastases based on superior neurocognitive preservation and equivalent survival [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 5, 7]
3. Surgery recommended for symptomatic/large lesions to enable rapid steroid tapering and optimize immunotherapy [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 4]
4. BRAF-targeted therapy reserved for BRAF-mutated patients, particularly those symptomatic or requiring high-dose steroids [Citation: EANO-ESMO_BM_Guideline.pdf, Page 7]
